What Music Bot Is

Music Bot is a small stand-alone machine that takes your mood and turns it into a printed playlist “receipt.” You pick a genre, aggression level, and explicit/clean preference on the LCD, and the ESP32 talks to an AI model to print out ten songs that match what you asked for.

Overview

This was an individual project built around a Personal Pet Peeve. Mine is getting completely tired of my playlists and then wasting time scrolling through music apps, skipping every song because nothing feels right. Music Bot is my way of offloading that decision fatigue into a small physical machine.

On the front of the box, a 16×2 LCD and rotary encoder walk you through three simple choices: genre, aggression level from 1–10, and whether explicit lyrics are okay. The ESP32 sends those settings to an AI model, then prints a ten-song recommendation list on a thermal receipt, turning music discovery into a little ritual instead of more screen time.

I also wanted the box to have personality, not just feel like another screen. A rotary encoder and 16×2 LCD give the interface a tactile, old-school menu vibe, and the thermal printer feels like a tiny ticket machine or gacha pull for new tracks. Inside, an ESP32 handles WiFi, talks to the OpenAI API, and streams the generated playlist text to the printer.

The project combines electronics, code, and fabrication: I wired the ESP32 to an LCD, rotary encoder, and high-current thermal printer, designed and laser-cut a wooden enclosure in Rhino, and debugged power and serial issues along the way. In the end, Music Bot turns the boring “what should I listen to?” problem into something playful and physical.

How It Works

User Flow

  • Turn the rotary encoder to scroll through genres on the LCD.
  • Press the encoder to lock in your genre.
  • Twist again to pick an aggression level from 1–10.
  • Press to confirm, then toggle between explicit / clean.
  • After a short pause, Music Bot prints a ten-song recommendation list.

Interaction Goals

The goal is to slow down recommendation, make it tactile, and detach it from a phone screen. The printed slip becomes something you can keep in your wallet, stick on a notebook, or hand to a friend.
